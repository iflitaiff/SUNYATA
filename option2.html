SUNYATA

Exploring the void within form

In Buddhist philosophy, Sunyata (emptiness) is the understanding that all phenomena are empty of inherent existence.

Everything that exists is in a constant state of flux, arising and passing away due to causes and conditions, never existing independently.

"Form is emptiness, emptiness is form. Emptiness is not separate from form, form is not separate from emptiness."
